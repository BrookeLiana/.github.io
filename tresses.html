Tresses

Mobile Application - Concept project

Tresses is a mobile application that allows users to search for and book a hairstylist within a certain vicinity of their current location, based off of their selected hair-type and desired hairstyle.

Problem

Going to a hair salon has become a luxury with busy, unpredictable schedules, children to watch and the increasing inconvenience of the time it takes to travel to a salon. Additionally, most mainstream salons do not have a wide range of styling knowledge for various hair types and the different kind of styling techniques that each of those different hair types require for maintaining healthy hair. Finally, there is a general lack of transparency and therefore no accountability held for hairstylists when working with clients.

Solution

Tresses is an application that allows users to search for and hire a hairstylist near their current location, who will travel to their home to provide hairstyling services based off of the users’ selected hair type. Tresses aims to provide an inclusive hairstyling experience for a wide range of hair types (straight to very curly), and to use hairstyling techniques that encourages healthy hair. Tresses also allows users to view potential hairstylists for hire, their credentials, reviews, ratings and profile information prior to confirming them.

User Research Highlights

93.3%

Competitive Analysis

An analysis of market competitors GlamSquad, beGlammed and ManeStream confirmed that Tresses would be a great addition that provides inclusive services for a wide range of hair types, and transparency regarding the credentials and resume of hairstylists. Neither of the examined competitors allowed for a user to input personal information regarding their hair type, or hair length, allowed the user to view a picture of the hair stylist who had been hired for service, offered a choice on the selection of hair stylist, or have any background or resume information available on the stylist.

User Personas

Three users with distinct backgrounds were interviewed to further understand their frustrations, motivations and goals.

Taryn G.

Taryn is a busy new mother and is unfortunately unable to visit the salon with her young child, and finds the time it takes to travel inconvenient.

Carry B.

Carry has had bad experiences with hairstylists who didn't know how to style and care for her curly hair, which resulted in hair breakage and damage.

Morgan T.

Morgan is a young professional who travels frequently, and has an unpredictable work schedule and never knows when she'll get off of work in the evening.

User Stories & Flows

A detailed flow diagram illustrated paths for new and returning users, booking a new hair appointment, selecting a stylist, selecting a hairstyle, inputting a current location and billing information and canceling an appointment. This comprehensive information architecture served as a template for the creation of the site.

Visual Design

Tresses is an application that is about inclusivity, flexibility, and women embracing their natural hair texture and having access to hairstyling no matter their hair type. The visual design of Tresses aims to reflect this and incorporates very natural tones, various shades of brown, terracotta, slate grey and utilizes a modern and minimalist typeface.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

Low fidelity wireframes were developed and tested through several iterations before they were finally ready to be developed with the visual design elements applied. Random users were selected to complete usability testing through an application called inVision.

Users were asked to complete certain tasks through the use of different context stories. A numbering system was implemented where users were asked to rate each of their given tasks ranging in difficulty to ease from 1-4. This helped to illuminate pain points in the design that had low ratings of 2 or below. Some of which included Canceling an appointment and logging out; both of these options were not present depending on which page a user was currently on. Another pain point was that there wasn’t a confirmation that an appointment had successfully been canceled.

Hi-Fi Wireframes

With the invaluable feedback I received from lo-fi usability testing, I implemented several changes that would make the user interaction and experience more straightforward, intuitive and enjoyable to use, and developed interactive prototypes using InVision and Flinto.

Conclusion

Tresses addresses key issues that women today face with increasing time and travel constraints, personal and work obligations, in addition to the general lack of inclusion for women with varying hair types and hair care needs. With the number of iterations it took to develop this mobile application into an interactive prototype with animations using both InVision and Flinto, I learned that the most minimal designs are often times the most complex and time consuming to design. Additionally, through conducting several preference tests, I was made aware that what I may consider to be an important implementation in a design flow, may not align with the users opinion of what is considered to be within the MVP scope.

If I were to continue development of this project, I would add more variety in the hairstyles offered, add the additional hair types that exist that were not included, develop the stylist user flows of viewing appointments and confirming clients, and have the user be able to favorite stylists, leave reviews and share their finished hairstyling experience via social media.
