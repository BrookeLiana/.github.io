UX DESIGNER, NYC

About me

My name is Brooke Hargrove, a former fashion assistant and publicist turned UX designer, currently based in New York City. I was born in Michigan, but grew up abroad in Germany, Thailand and the UAE. I graduated with a liberal arts degree in Anthropology from Mount Holyoke College with an affinity for studying and conducting field research to further understand human behavior and how it evolves over time across various cultures.

Since graduating, I’ve worked at leading fashion companies and the exposure has fine-tuned my eye for beautifully crafted design. In an effort to combine my love for fashion and design and the social science of Anthropology, I was naturally drawn to UX design. I love finding problems, and collaboratively creating elegant solutions for an effortless, intuitive experience.

Other than UX, I love traveling, Audrey Hepburn films (My Fair Lady is a favorite), photography, food, music, reading, and art museums.

What I do

Discovery

Define the project parameters and clarify goals and objectives through having a series of conversations with key stakeholders and target audience. Conduct interviews to explore potential opportunities for improving user interaction and optimizing user satisfaction with the product.

Information Architecture

Outline requirements for project and define a plan for building through research analysis, a design brief and competitive analysis. Create, organize, structure and test all content for the site by developing user stories, user flows, sitemaps, lo-fi wireframes, prototypes and usability tests.

Design

Determine and implement the visual direction through creating sketches, mindmaps, mood boards, style guides, hi-fi mockups and preference tests.

Code

Create a testable version of the product that is near final form by creating interactive prototypes and coding the HTML & CSS needed.
